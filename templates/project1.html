üìò Overview

This data analytics project investigates customer shopping behavior using transactional data from 3,900 purchases across multiple product categories. The goal is to uncover insights into spending patterns, customer segmentation, product preferences, and subscription behaviors to support data-driven marketing and product strategies.

Key Business Question: ‚ÄúHow can the company leverage consumer shopping data to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimize marketing and product strategies?‚Äù

üìÇ Datasets

Source: POS System | File: customer_shopping_behavior.csv | Rows: 3,900 | Columns: 18

  • Customer Demographics: age, gender, location, subscription status
  • Purchase Details: item purchased, category, purchase amount, size, color, season
  • Behavioral Data: discount applied, previous purchases, frequency, review rating, shipping type

Data Quality Summary: Missing Values: 37 (in review ratings). Data types cleaned and standardized for consistency.

üîç Project Steps

  1. Business Understanding: Defined 10 key business questions and analytical goals aligned with business strategy.
  2. Data Exploration (EDA): Loaded dataset into Python, conducted descriptive stats, visualized distributions.
  3. Data Cleaning & Preparation: Handled missing values, standardized columns, created new features, integrated dataset into SQLite.
  4. SQL-Based Analysis: Executed queries on revenue, high-spending users, top products, age-group revenue, subscriber vs non-subscriber spending.
  5. Insight Generation: Key findings include female customers driving 68% of revenue, loyal customers dominating sales, top-rated products identified.
  6. Data Visualization: Developed interactive Tableau dashboard for revenue, segmentation, product trends, and subscription analysis.
  7. Recommendations: Boost subscriptions, reward repeat buyers, optimize discounts, targeted marketing for high-performing demographics.
